The key to vegan baking? The right substitute

Using the right substitute for the common non-vegan baking ingredients (like butter, eggs, and milk) is key to a good bake, but don't worry you can make amazing (if not better) bakes using plant-based ingredients. Here are all my favoruite substitues and when to use them!

Flax eggs are made from warm water and ground flax seed, they are the perfect egg substitute when baking cakes and scones, anything with a more crumbly texture.

Another substistue for eggs, the chia egg is made in the same way as the flax egg are with warm water and chia seed but the chia egg works great in bread doughs (especially enriched doughs).

Cornstarch is the perfect subsitite for eggs when you want to make custard or curd (to put on some lovely buns), it works a treat to thicken the mixture without overhwlming the flavaour because of its neutral taste.

Mashed banana and applesauce are both good substitues for eggs as they can be used as a binding agent, you can also use these as a sub for butter if you dont have venga margerine on hand.

Aquafaba is most well-known as a perfect substitute for whipped eggwhites and making meringue, but as an egg white substitute it can be used to make light fluffy cakes, macarons and even choux pastry for eclairs.

Vegan margerine is the easiest sub for butter but not always readily available, if you dont have vegan margerine, you can sub coconut oil, vegtables oils, or nut butter (or a combination or all). Make sure to adjust your quantities here.
